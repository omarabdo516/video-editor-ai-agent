## 4. Caption / typography system

The reel uses **at least 6 distinct caption treatments** that cycle through the body zone:

### 4.1 Yellow handwritten / cursive (hook only)
- **When:** t=0-2 (intro hook)
- **Look:** Bright yellow Arabic in a script-like display font, ~80px tall, no background
- **Example:** "وحوش الحسابات في مصر"
- **Purpose:** Immediate visual hook — distinctive personality, hand-crafted feel

### 4.2 Massive bold display (title cards)
- **When:** t=3-7 (intro title) + t=67-73 (outro bookend)
- **Look:** "المحاسب الشامل" 2-line, ~140px tall, white bold geometric Arabic, **yellow horizontal underline accent under "الشامل"** (~20px tall, ~80% of word width)
- **Purpose:** Workshop brand mark + bookend for memorability

### 4.3 Number-integrated keyword pop
- **When:** t=2-7 (intro), reused for "365" at t=26
- **Look:** Number in MASSIVE yellow embedded INSIDE Arabic word — `س2026نة` reads as "سنة" with "2026" filling the body of the word
- **Purpose:** Make a key statistic UNMISSABLE while keeping it grammatically integrated

### 4.4 Translucent navy pill
- **When:** Throughout chapter content, especially key value statements
- **Look:** White Arabic text inside a blue/navy translucent capsule (~20% opacity navy fill, soft rounded corners), text ~60px
- **Examples:** "في الورشة دي بيبقى عندنا تارجت" / "يقدر يعمل اقفالات وتسويات" / "طبقاً ل IFRS 18 اللي هيبدا تطبيقه"
- **Purpose:** Anchored, scan-stable caption for key claims — eyes settle on it

### 4.5 Plain white floating
- **When:** Conversational statements, secondary content
- **Look:** Pure white bold Arabic, ~50-60px, no background, soft shadow/glow for legibility
- **Examples:** "اللي مطلوب منك" / "هنعمل عرض للقوائم المالية" / "السلام عليكم ورحمة الله"
- **Purpose:** Lighter visual weight than pill — for filler / connective speech

### 4.6 Single-word pop
- **When:** Short emphatic moments in chapter content
- **Look:** ONE word HUGE (~100px), white floating, body zone, sometimes with subtle shadow
- **Examples:** "من" (t=22), "اي" (t=38), "انك" (t=51), "وده" (t=30)
- **Purpose:** Visual punctuation matching speaker's vocal emphasis — micro-attention beats

### 4.7 Inline keyword highlight (the Hormozi pattern)
- **When:** Multi-word phrases where one word is the key
- **Look:** Mostly white text with the keyword in YELLOW + slight outline
- **Examples:** "من اول **القيد** / لغاية **القوائم**" (t=23) / "خلال الـ **365** يوم في السنة" (t=26)
- **Purpose:** Same as our [WordCaption.tsx](../../../src/components/WordCaption.tsx) — keyword draws the eye while context provides meaning

**Color-treatment rule observed:** Numbers, dates, technical terms (Arabic), and Latin acronyms (IFRS 18) ALL get the YELLOW treatment. Plain connectors stay white.

## 5. The chapter overlay system (the master pattern)

The reel's spine is a **numbered chapter index** that builds anticipation set up by the t=16 "اربع محاور رئيسية" announcement.

```
│                       │
│   [Chapter title]     │  ← yellow bold Arabic
│   [Optional subtitle] │  ← smaller white Arabic

```

### 5.2 Chapter inventory

| # | Time | Title | Subtitle | Numeral system | Notes |
|---|------|-------|----------|----------------|-------|
| 1 | 20-27s | الدورة المحاسبية | (none) | Latin "1" | Single-line title |
| 2 | 28-39s | بناء ادارة الحسابات | بناء نظام الحسابات للشركات | Latin "2" | Two-tier title |
| 3 | 40-53s | التشغيل | (none) | Latin "3" | Single-word title |
| 4 | 54-66s | الإقفالات واعداد القوائم المالية | (none) | **Arabic-Indic "٤"** ⚠️ | 2-line wrap, INCONSISTENT numeral |

### 5.3 Chapter reveal animation

Sequential, 2-step:
1. **Step A (frame 1):** Badge appears alone at top-center (~y=100-200). No label yet.
2. **Step B (frame 2+):** Label slides in (RTL motion), badge re-positions slightly to make room.

### 5.4 Chapter transition animation (between chapters)

**Vertical card stack with cross-fade** (NOT horizontal slide):
- Old chapter (badge + label) shrinks/fades **upward**
- New chapter rises from behind the old, also fading in
- Mid-transition (~1 second): both layers visible simultaneously, semi-transparent
- The badge appears to do a 3D card flip during this moment (frames at t=40, t=55 captured this state)

### 5.5 Chapter persistence

- Each chapter overlay STAYS on screen for the full duration (7-12 seconds)
- Captions in the body zone CYCLE through styles WITHIN a single chapter (4-5 caption treatments per chapter)
- This creates **macro-stability** (chapter doesn't change) + **micro-variety** (caption changes every 1-3s)

## 7. Motion & pacing

- **Visual events:** ~25 distinct caption/overlay changes in 73.8s = **event every ~3s** (matches RS [feedback/style_evolution.md](../../../feedback/style_evolution.md) preference of "event every ~4s" — slightly denser, very close to our target)
- **Audio emphasis match-up:** 25 audio emphasis points + 25 visual events = visual editor is HIGHLY synchronized to speech rhythm
- **Camera dynamics:** ~8-10 implicit shot changes (tight↔medium↔wide) without hard cuts. Disguised as natural conversation framing.
- **Transitions:** All cross-fades or slide-ins. NO hard cuts, NO whip-pans, NO flash transitions. The reel feels SMOOTH despite high information density.
- **Speed:** Comfortable medium-fast — lets viewer process each caption before next change.

## 8. Retention tricks observed

1. **Build-up brand stack** (t=0→8): Don't dump all branding at once. Hook with minimal stack, layer in branding gradually as viewer commits.
2. **Number-integrated reveal** (t=2-7): The "2026" inside "سنة" makes the reader's brain pause to parse it — micro-engagement spike.
3. **Anticipation announcement** (t=16-19): "اربع محاور رئيسية" tells the viewer "you're about to see 4 things" → creates an open loop that the chapter overlays close one-by-one.
4. **Numbered chapters as progress bar** (t=20-66): Implicit "we're at 2 of 4" feel keeps viewers watching to completion.
5. **Bookend title** (t=4 ↔ t=67): Title appears MASSIVE at intro, shrinks to badge during content, RETURNS to MASSIVE at outro. Visual rhyme = memorability.
6. **Caption stacking** (t=18-19): New caption lines APPEND below older ones rather than swap. Creates "growing proposition" feel.
7. **Dramatic pause exploitation**: 5 detected dramatic pauses → these are likely where the chapter overlays TRANSITION. Audio dip + visual change = reset attention.

## 9. Brand fit verdict for RS

### 9.1 Direct steal candidates (high alignment with RS)

✅ **Numbered chapter overlay system** — PERFECT for RS's workshop content (which has natural chapter structure: workshop modules, accounting steps, etc.). The "1, 2, 3, 4" badges + Arabic title is highly applicable.

✅ **Yellow accent palette** — The yellow used is essentially identical to RS Accent Gold #FFB501. Already on-brand.

✅ **Caption style cycling within scene** — Our current system uses ONE caption style per render. This reel proves that CYCLING styles within a single reel (pop / pill / plain / keyword highlight) feels rich rather than chaotic.

✅ **Build-up brand stack pattern** — Don't show logo + workshop title at t=0. Reveal them gradually 5-8s in. Higher hook attention.

✅ **Bookend title pattern** — Massive title at intro + same massive title at outro. Already implementable with our existing scene system.

✅ **Anticipation announcement** — "We'll cover X things" before showing the X chapter overlays. This is a Phase 5/6 content-analysis pattern we can adopt.

✅ **Audio-synced visual events** — 25 visual events ↔ 25 audio emphasis points. Our [audio_energy.json](../../../scripts/audio_energy.py) data is the right input for this; we should verify our planner is using it for visual event placement.

### 9.2 Adapt-with-changes

🔧 **Chapter transition animation (card stack)** — Beautiful but custom. We could implement as a new entrance/exit preset on [FullScreenScene.tsx](../../../src/components/scenes/FullScreenScene.tsx). Or simpler: cross-fade between persistent overlays.

🔧 **Number-integrated word reveal** ("2026" inside "سنة") — Works visually but is custom typography. Could be a special caption variant in [WordCaption.tsx](../../../src/components/WordCaption.tsx) called `numberIntegrated` for stat-heavy reels.

🔧 **Translucent navy pill captions** — Easy to add as a new caption variant alongside hormozi/pop. Probably worth a dedicated `WordCaptionPill.tsx` component.

🔧 **Bilingual captions** (Arabic + Latin acronym, e.g., "IFRS 18") — Already supported by our font stack (Alexandria + IBM Plex Sans Arabic both have Latin subsets). Just need to ensure the planner doesn't strip Latin tokens.

### 9.3 Skip / off-brand

❌ **Yellow handwritten / cursive caption font** (hook at t=0-2) — Off-brand for RS (we use Alexandria, not script fonts). Skip this style.

❌ **Inconsistent numerals** (chapters 1-3 Latin, chapter 4 Arabic-Indic) — Probably an editor mistake. RS BRAND.md mandates "الأرقام إنجليزية" (English numerals). We MUST be consistent.

❌ **5-layer brand stack with two top logos + footer stamp** — Too much branding for RS's cleaner aesthetic. We use 2 layers (logo bug + lower third). Adopting this 5-layer approach would clutter our reels.

❌ **Real-set background (curtain + lamp + plants)** — RS reels are typically rendered, not physical-set. We don't need to replicate this; our solid color tokens + motion bg work fine.
